Wow! What can I say? ITZY have announced the start of summer with their highest-energy track for several years. 'Motto' delivers all-time classic K-Pop vibes, but with ITZY's signature relentless energy, bordering on aggression! They are giving you no choice but to sing along and enjoy it. ITZY have taken the 3rd gen dance-pop/synth-pop formula and added plenty of 4th gen spice and sparkle.

The arrangement can be best described as 'big'. Big synths, big drums, soaring vocals and big track length! It's a cliche at this point: but a song with pre- and post- choruses, a bridge, and over 3 mins long, in this economy?! Outrageous. The vocals are well-woven, with each of the members voices highlighted while also changing quickly between one another without losing momentum. Yeji's line "Take me there like that" at the end of the bridge will send a shiver down your spine.

The music video delivers a change of pace from the melancholy of more recent ITZY MVs 'Imaginary Friend', 'Girls Will be Girls', and 'Tunnel Vision'. 'Motto' returns to the colourful stylings of 'Dalla Dalla' or 'Wannabe', but with a more sophisticated and mature pastel palette. 

A bit of a deep cut, but the line "Take me to a better place, 이 결말 따윈 not afraid" reminds me of the line from Savage Garden's 'To the Moon and Back': "But somewhere in a private place, She packs her bags for outer space"

The mini album has two other group tracks, and solos from each member. I was lucky enough to hear all these solos live at their concert in Sydney in April 2026, so I knew these were coming. (Shame 'Motto' wasn't there! I'll just have to go to their next tour!) I love Yuna's city-synth-pop 'Tangerine' so much I'd been listening to it on fan phone recordings, so it's great to have the real version out now. Chaeryeong's 'Undefined' delivers a groovy brass R&B sound, and Lia sings 'Asylum', a pop-ballad in English with singer-songwriter vibes (understandable since MRCH was on the credits for it).

The 3rd gen K-Pop sound is back, and better than ever! Hopefully, we see some more of this, not just from ITZY but from others too. Now stop reading and enjoy this track!
