While I loved all the solo stage singles from the ITZY concert, the one that stood out the most was Yuna's 'Tangerine'. This is a tasty city pop / synth pop song that indeed sounds likes it has been freshly picked from the citrus fields of Jeju Island.

This song blends classic city pop progression with rich layers of synth pads and arpeggios from synth pop. While there isn't a bridge, there's a delightful synth solo section. The relatively fast tempo delivers a higher energy than your average city pop track.

It's also interesting to note it was written, composed and arranged by Sim Eunji who is behind many JYP songs including several masterpieces from Twice ('Yes or Yes', 'I can't stop me', 'Scientist') and ITZY ('That's a No No'). She was born in 1984, which is of course the year 'Plastic Love' was released, so it was inevitable that she'd write a city pop piece!
